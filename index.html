Dr. Norman Borlaug

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist and humanitarian who led initiatives to combat world hunger. He was born on March 25, 1914, and passed away on September 12, 2009.

Throughout his career, Borlaug developed high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties that significantly increased food production, particularly in developing countries. His work is credited with saving over a billion lives and earning him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970.
